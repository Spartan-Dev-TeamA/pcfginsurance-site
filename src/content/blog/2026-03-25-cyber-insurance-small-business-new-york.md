## What Cyber Insurance Covers

Cyber insurance protects your business from financial losses related to:

**First-Party Coverage:**
- **Data breach response** — Costs for notifying affected customers, credit monitoring services, and public relations
- **Ransomware attacks** — Payment demands and recovery costs (some policies cover ransom; many advise against paying)
- **Business interruption** — Lost income when systems are down
- **Cyber extortion** — Professional negotiators and payment coverage
- **Hardware replacement** — Costs to replace compromised computers and devices

**Third-Party Coverage:**
- **Legal defense** — If customers or partners sue over a data breach
- **Regulatory fines** — New York has specific cybersecurity regulations with penalties
- **Media liability** — Copyright infringement, defamation claims from online content

## 2026 Cyber Insurance Requirements for NY Businesses

The cyber insurance landscape has changed dramatically. Here's what New York businesses need to know:

### Carrier Requirements Are Stricter

Most cyber insurance carriers now require:

- **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** on all systems
- **Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)** on all devices
- **Tested backup restores** — proof you can actually recover data
- **Documented incident response plans** — written procedures for breach response

Without these basics, getting coverage is difficult—or expensive.

### New York Regulatory Requirements

New York's Department of Financial Services (DFS) has implemented strict cybersecurity regulations. Additionally, the **NY SHIELD Act** requires:

- Reasonable safeguards to protect private information
- Breach notification within reasonable time (often 30-60 days)
- Written security policies and risk assessments

If you handle any New York resident's personal data, you're subject to these requirements.

### Contract Requirements from Clients

If you do business with larger companies, they've likely added cybersecurity requirements to your contracts:

- Minimum cyber insurance limits ($1M is common)
- Proof of security measures
- Right to audit your systems

## Average Cyber Insurance Costs for NY Small Businesses

Costs vary based on:

- Business size and revenue
- Industry (healthcare, finance, retail face higher rates)
- Data sensitivity (credit cards, health records = higher risk)
- Security measures in place
- Claims history

**Typical costs for small businesses:**
- $1M coverage: $1,500-$3,000/year
- $2M coverage: $2,500-$5,000/year

New York businesses typically pay 10-20% more than the national average due to higher regulatory requirements and risk profiles.

## What Cyber Threats Are NY Businesses Facing?

### Top Threats in 2026:

1. **Ransomware** — Attacks increased 150% for small businesses. Criminals target businesses they believe will pay quickly.

2. **Business Email Compromise (BEC)** — Scammers impersonate executives or vendors to wire money. Average loss: $120,000.

3. **Phishing** — Fake emails trick employees into revealing passwords or clicking malicious links.

4. **Supply Chain Attacks** — Hackers compromise your vendors to get to your systems.

5. **Cryptojacking** — Criminals use your computers to mine cryptocurrency, slowing your systems.

### Industries Most Affected in New York:

- Healthcare (strictly regulated, valuable data)
- Financial services (high-value targets)
- Retail (credit card data)
- Professional services (client data)
- Manufacturing (intellectual property)

## Do You Really Need Cyber Insurance?

Answer these questions:

1. Do you store customer personal information (names, emails, addresses)?
2. Do you process credit card payments?
3. Do you have employees who access company systems remotely?
4. Do you rely on computers for daily operations?
5. Would a week of system downtime hurt your business?

If you answered "yes" to 3 or more, you need cyber insurance.
