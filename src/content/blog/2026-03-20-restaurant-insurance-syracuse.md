## Why Restaurants Need Specialized Insurance

Restaurants face unique risks that general business insurance doesn't fully address:

- **Food service hazards** — slips, burns, foodborne illness
- **Liquor liability** — over-serving patrons
- **High employee turnover** — workers' comp claims
- **Equipment breakdown** — walk-ins, ovens
- **Business interruption** — forced closures

---

## Types of Restaurant Insurance

### General Liability
**Essential for every restaurant.**

Covers:
- Customer injuries (slip-and-fall)
- Food contamination claims
- Property damage caused by your operations
- Advertising injury

### Workers' Compensation
**Required in New York** for any employee.

Covers:
- Medical costs for workplace injuries
- Lost wages during recovery
- Disability benefits

Common restaurant injuries:
- Burns from kitchen equipment
- Slips on wet floors
- Cuts from knives
- Back injuries from lifting

### Liquor Liability (Dram Shop)
**Critical if you serve alcohol.**

Covers:
- Injuries caused by intoxicated patrons
- Property damage from drunk patrons
- Legal defense costs

**New York Dram Shop Act** makes restaurants liable for serving visibly intoxicated patrons who then cause harm.

### Commercial Property
Covers:
- Building (if you own)
- Kitchen equipment
- Furniture and fixtures
- Inventory (food, liquor, supplies)

### Business Interruption
Covers lost income when you can't operate due to:
- Fire
- Equipment breakdown
- Storm damage
- Other covered events

### Food Contamination
Covers:
- Cost to replace contaminated food
- Lost revenue during closure
- Cleanup costs

### Employment Practices Liability (EPL)
Covers:
- Wrongful termination
- Discrimination
- Sexual harassment
- Wage and hour disputes

## FAQ: Restaurant Insurance

**Q: Does my restaurant need liquor liability if I don't serve hard liquor?**
A: Yes. Any establishment serving alcohol needs liquor liability. Beer and wine can also lead to intoxicated patron issues.

**Q: What happens if someone gets food poisoning at my restaurant?**
A: General liability covers food contamination claims. Food contamination coverage adds extra protection.

**Q: How much workers' comp do I need?**
A: New York requires workers' comp for all employees. Cost depends on payroll, industry code, and claims history.

**Q: Can I get insurance for a food truck?**
A: Yes. Food trucks need similar coverage to restaurants, with some modifications for the mobile nature.
